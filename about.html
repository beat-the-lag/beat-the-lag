What is Jet Lag?

Jet lag is a temporary disorder that affects people who travel across multiple time zones. It is a sleep disorder and is caused by the fact that during this type of travel, one's internal clock and the local time of the placed traveled to do not match.

When a person is jet lagged, they often report feeling drowsy, tired, irritable, and disoriented. Often times, it may take multiple days to recover from this.


Ways to Fight Jet Lag

One of the best ways to prevent jet lag from happening is to gradually familiarize yourself, and your body, with the local time of your travel destination. Experts recommend shifting your sleep time by around an hour every day for a number of days before your travel until you are matched up with the destination's time.

Other factors also come into play when you are trying to reduce the effects such as jet lag such alcohol and caffeine consumption, as well as if you sleep during your commute.
